Home

Human activities are significantly altering the Earth's environment, especially freshwater systems. This includes changes in natural flow patterns and the depletion of groundwater resources. Climate change adds to these challenges. My research employs quantitative modeling to integrate climate, societal, and agricultural factors for assessing water resources and environmental impacts. Over time, my research has become more interdisciplinary, involving stakeholders, socio-economic dimensions, and data science. The focus is on addressing complex issues related to water, climate change, energy, and infrastructure resilience. This approach aims to understand these interactions better and advance water cycle studies.

Join us

  • Graduate Student Opportunity:
  • We are looking for enthusiastic and highly motivated PhD students to join our group in starting in the coming Spring or Fall semester. Students should have bachelor and masters degrees in Civil Engineering, Environmental Engineering, Geoscience, or a closely related field, and an interest to work in the area of large-scale hydrological modeling, climate change, and water resources and agricultural sustainability. For more information, please feel free to check our research areas, current projects, recent publications, and also the admission requirements.

  • Ideal candidates are expected to have a strong background in hydrology and computational modeling and expertise in data analysis and computer programming using Fortran, Python, or similar programming languages. Strong past academic record (undergraduate and MS GPA) and test (GRE, TOEFL/IELTS) scores are expected.
